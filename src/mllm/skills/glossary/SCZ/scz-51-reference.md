# Neuroscience Schizophrenia Theories -- Glossary Reference
# Role: neuroscientist

This document defines the 51 biophysical factors used to evaluate theories of Schizophrenia circuit deficits (SCZ-51), organized into three 17-factor hypothesis groups. Each factor is evaluated on a symmetric scale from **+1.0 (Supporting Supporting Pole)** to **-1.0 (Supporting Opposing Pole)**.

**Tag Legend**

- **Quantitative** -- Factor is measured with continuous or numerical evidence.
- **Qualitative** -- Factor is assessed by presence/absence or categorical judgment.
- **Methodological** -- Factor relates to the method of observation rather than the mechanism itself.

**Context Legend**

- **GABA/Local** -- Focus on local microcircuitry and interneuron dynamics.
- **Hierarchical/Systemic** -- Focus on brain-wide connectivity and large-scale scaling.
- **Symptomatic** -- Focus on behavioral outcomes and clinical symptom mapping.

## H1: GABAergic Etiology (IDs 1--17)

Factors in this group address whether the primary etiology is a GABAergic/Interneuron deficit (+1) or an alternative mechanism (e.g., Glutamatergic, Dopaminergic, or Glial) (-1).

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag | Contexts |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|----------|
| 1 | PV mRNA Reduction | (+) Evidence of primary Parvalbumin mRNA reduction; (-) PV expression remains intact at baseline. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 2 | GAD67 Deficit | (+) Evidence of primary GAD67 reduction; (-) GAD67 expression remains intact. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 3 | GABA-Pacing Failure | (+) Evidence of primary oscillatory pacing failure due to interneuron deficit; (-) Pacing failure driven by non-GABAergic noise. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 4 | SST-Gating Deficit | (+) Evidence of primary Somatostatin-mediated gating failure; (-) Dendritic gating intact or failure driven by distal input. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 5 | GAT-1 Downregulation | (+) Presynaptic GABAergic transporter reduction; (-) GAT-1 expression baseline/intact. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 6 | PV-Basket Cell Density | (+) Physical loss of PV+ interneuron population; (-) PV cell counts are normal. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 7 | Perisomatic Shunting Gain | (+) Reduced PV-mediated shunting effectiveness; (-) Shunting gain remains at normal/compensatory levels. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 8 | Chloride Polarity Shift | (+) Pathological GABAergic depolarization (KCC2/NKCC1 imbalance); (-) GABA remains strictly inhibitory. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 9 | VIP Disinhibition Gain | (+) Pathological VIP-mediated release of E-cells; (-) VIP-mediated disinhibition remains regulated. | Qualitative | GABA/Local |
| 10 | GABA-A Subunit Shift | (+) Pathological shift in alpha1 postsynaptic receptor density; (-) Receptor composition baseline/stable. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 11 | CCK-Basket Dysregulation | (+) Altered activity of CCK-positive basket cells; (-) CCK-pacing remains intact. | Qualitative | GABA/Local |
| 12 | PV-Pyr Synaptic Conductance | (+) Direct reduction in PV-to-Pyramidal synaptic strength ($g_{max}$); (-) Synaptic strength intact or increased. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 13 | Interneuron Recruitment Delay | (+) Systematic delay in GABAergic response to drive; (-) Recruitment timing remains synchronous. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 14 | E/I Ratio Volatility | (+) Fluctuations driven primarily by GABAergic failure; (-) Volatility driven by excitatory/modulatory noise. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 15 | Interneuron Metabolic Stress | (+) High-metabolic demand leading to interneuron-specific oxidative damage; (-) Metabolic stress is general or non-GABAergic. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 16 | Basket Cell Jitter | (+) Increased temporal jitter in basket cell rhythmic firing; (-) Rhythmic firing remains precise. | Quantitative | GABA/Local |
| 17 | GABAergic Structural Plasticity | (+) Evidence of primary remodeling of GABAergic terminals; (-) Structural plasticity is strictly glutamatergic. | Qualitative | GABA/Local |

## H2: Systemic Scaling (IDs 18--34)

Factors in this group address whether the pathology is ubiquitous/brain-wide (+1) or localized/sparse (-1) across the brain and symptoms.

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag | Contexts |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|----------|
| 18 | MMN Blunting Ubiquity | (+) Evidence of MMN amplitude reduction brain-wide; (-) MMN deficit restricted to a single area (e.g., A1). | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 19 | Brain-Wide Beta Elevation | (+) Elevate baseline beta power across all cortical areas; (-) Beta elevation restricted to specific frontal/parietal hubs. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 20 | Hierarchical Disconnectivity | (+) Global failure of top-down and bottom-up signaling; (-) Disconnectivity restricted to a single hierarchical level. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 21 | Universal CSD Deficit | (+) Consistent laminar current source density (CSD) profiles across areas; (-) CSD profiles show area-specific variations. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 22 | Multisensory Gating Failure | (+) Deficits in gating for all sensory modalities (Auditory, Visual, Somatosensory); (-) Gating failure is modality-specific. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 23 | Global Gray Matter Loss | (+) Diffuse reduction in cortical thickness brain-wide; (-) Gray matter loss is focal (e.g., Temporal lobe only). | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 24 | Thalamocortical Dysconnectivity | (+) Global failure of thalamic relay to all cortical areas; (-) Dysconnectivity focal to the MD thalamus/PFC link. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 25 | Interspecies Consistency | (+) Deficits present across diverse animal models and humans; (-) Deficits are species-specific or model-specific. | Qualitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 26 | Universal Gain Instability | (+) Instability of signal gain across all cortical columns; (-) Gain instability is focal to the PFC. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 27 | Global Synchrony Breakdown | (+) Loss of long-range coherence between distant brain areas; (-) Synchrony breakdown is localized/short-range. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 28 | Ubiquitous NMDAR Hypofunction | (+) Evidence of NMDAR deficit in all hierarchical levels; (-) NMDAR hypofunction localized to specific deep layers. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 29 | Whole-Brain Network Latency | (+) Consistent increase in response latency across the brain; (-) Latency shifts are focal to the sensory periphery. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 30 | Cross-Scale Scaling | (+) Deficits scale identically from single units to LFP brain-wide; (-) Scaling is inconsistent across different brain levels. | Methodological | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 31 | State-Independent Deficit | (+) Deficit persists across all brain states (Wake, Sleep, Anesthesia); (-) Deficit is state-dependent (e.g., wake-only). | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 32 | Global White Matter Integrity | (+) Diffuse reduction in fractional anisotropy brain-wide; (-) White matter loss is restricted to specific fasciculi. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 33 | Ubiquitous Oxidative Stress | (+) Evidence of oxidative damage across all interneuron populations brain-wide; (-) Stress localized to PV+ neurons. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |
| 34 | Global Information Entropy | (+) Consistent increase in signal entropy across all brain regions; (-) Entropy increase is focal and symptom-linked. | Quantitative | Hierarchical/Systemic |

## H3: Symptomatic Unity (IDs 35--51)

Factors in this group address whether schizophrenia is pathologically uniform (+1) or symtomatically separated (-1).

| ID | Factor Name | Definition | Tag | Contexts |
|----|-------------|------------|-----|----------|
| 35 | Unitary Pathological Origin | (+) Core deficit (e.g., PV loss) explains all symptoms; (-) Distinct circuit drivers for Positive vs. Negative symptoms. | Qualitative | Symptomatic |
| 36 | Positive/Negative Synergy | (+) Positive and Negative symptoms covary with the same biophysical marker; (-) Symptoms dissociate biologically. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 37 | Cognitive/Positive Linkage | (+) Cognitive deficits and hallucinations emerge from the same circuit failure; (-) They occur via independent mechanisms. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 38 | Unitary Neurochemical Driver | (+) Single modulatory deficit (e.g., DA) explains all symptom clusters; (-) Distinct modulatory origins (DA, 5-HT, ACh). | Qualitative | Symptomatic |
| 39 | Uniform Treatment Response | (+) All symptom clusters respond identically to the same intervention; (-) Selective response (e.g., meds help only positive symptoms). | Qualitative | Symptomatic |
| 40 | Common Genetic Risk | (+) Core symptoms driven by the same polygenic risk score; (-) Symptom domains mapped to distinct genetic clusters. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 41 | Unitary Plasticity Deficit | (+) Same failure of synaptic plasticity explains all behavioral deficits; (-) Distinct plasticity deficits for each domain. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 42 | Integrated Information Breakdown | (+) Failure of global information integration drives all symptoms; (-) Symptoms arise from modular failures. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 43 | Temporal Co-occurrence | (+) All symptoms emerge simultaneously during the illness course; (-) Staggered onset (e.g., cognitive before positive). | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 44 | Unitary Sensory/Cognitive Link | (+) Sensory gating and high-level cognitive deficits map to the same marker; (-) They are biologically dissociated. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 45 | Shared Metabolic Etiology | (+) All symptoms emerge from a unified metabolic failure; (-) Symptom-specific metabolic profiles. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 46 | Unitary Structural Predictor | (+) Single structural marker (e.g., PFC volume) predicts all symptoms; (-) Symptom-specific structural predictors. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 47 | Common Behavioral Phenotype | (+) Deficits across domains show identical statistical structure; (-) Domains show distinct behavioral patterns. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 48 | Global Gating Covariance | (+) Deficits in gating for all modalities correlate with total symptom severity; (-) Gating correlates only with specific symptoms. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 49 | Shared Inflammatory Profile | (+) Unified cytokine profile across all symptom domains; (-) Domain-specific inflammatory markers. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 50 | Unitary Latency Predictor | (+) Brain-wide response latency predicts all symptom categories; (-) Latency predicts only specific cognitive symptoms. | Quantitative | Symptomatic |
| 51 | Holistic Circuit Failure | (+) The system fails as a unified whole across all functions; (-) System fails as a collection of independent modules. | Qualitative | Symptomatic |
